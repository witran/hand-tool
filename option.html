Tips and Tricks

  • To go to this option page, Right Click to icon on the right of browser's address bar, and click 'Options'.
  • Turn Hand Tool on or off by Left Clicking the icon.
  • For Windows/Linux Laptop Touchpad users, turn on Touchpad Suggestion, which will use the Control Key as activator and try:
    • Rest one of your hand beside the touchpad.
    • Slide your finger up and down.
      You don't have to raise your finger up from the Touch Pad.
    • Hold and Release the Control Key with the other hand.
      Think of the Control Key as a scroll toggle, toggle it when your finger is sliding in the right direction.
  • For Stylus users, consider a higher scrolling speed. If there is a Left Mouse or Middle Mouse simulation button on the body of your Stylus (Wacom Stylus for example), then use it for activation, or you can also use Control/Command Key.
  • For Windows Mouse users, panning using Right Mouse Button is very helpful.
  • All Right Mouse Support is available in Chrome on Windows only.
  • By default, Linux users can activate panning by Middle Mouse.
  • If your Middle Mouse Wheel that is unable to scroll, but is able to click, it's time to toggle the Middle Mouse activation.
  • For Tablet users, consider using Chrome's native touch support.
  • 'Scrolling Speed is 1.5' means when your mouse moves 1 pixel, your screen scrolls 1.5 pixels.
  • You can change the speed by clicking the Number Input, or the +, - signs at the Settings Panel.

About

  • Hand Tool makes scrolling any webpage similar to:
    • Scrolling a smart phone, or
    • Photoshop Hand Tool, or
    • Google Map Panning Hand, or
    • Any Pdf Reader's Hand Tool, like Foxit...
  • It gives you a helpful alternative to scroll a webpage,
    more natural, intuitive, convenient, smooth, flexible and touch-friendly.
  • This extension was not built to replace other Chrome's default scrolling methods. Instead, it's a complement.
